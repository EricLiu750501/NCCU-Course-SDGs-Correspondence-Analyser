##  課程簡介Course Description
Sociology is the science and the art of understanding the connection between human behavior, social relationships, and the society we live in, what C. Wright Mills called “the sociological imagination.” As a comprehensive introduction to the discipline, this course will teach you the approach and foci of this unique perspective, as well as its practical value.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
When you finish this course, you will be able to:
  * Articulate the sociological imagination and engage it to examine topics of interest to sociologists including social construction, culture, the self, dimensions of difference, and social institutions. This examination will take the form of written assignments, exams, questions embedded in lectures, and engagement in discussions.
  * Master a specific strategy for critical thinking. You will practice this skill by using this framework to analyze widely-held assumptions both alone and in groups throughout the term.
  * Adopt a perspective oriented toward lifelong learning and the interdisciplinary application of sociology, having gained the confidence and skill to interpret social life through a sociological lens in both formal and more conversational settings.
