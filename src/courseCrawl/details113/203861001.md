##  課程簡介Course Description
The advent of the AI era has redefined higher education and competitiveness. There is a growing consensus among higher education experts that the competitiveness that today's college students should possess is the knowledge integrated from multiple disciplines, rather than the competitiveness built on a single professional knowledge that was emphasized in the past. In addition to strengthening students' professional competence in South Korea, this course provides an in-depth exploration of South Korea’s relations with five regions of the Americas, the Indo-Pacific, the European Union, the Middle East, and Africa, as well as trends in their interactions. 
Taught in English, this course is designed to exploreSouth Korea's foreign relations in a comprehensive way, economic diplomacy in particular, from South Korea's perspective and priorities. This course begins with the evolution of South Korea's foreign policy since 1948, with a focus on _the emergence of Han River Miracle in the 1970s_ , _the re-emergence of South Korea in the post-1997 Asian financial crisis_,_its successful FTA-centered economic diplomacy,_ prospects and challenges  _in the 21 st century_. Then, South Korea's foreign relations with a wide range of regions will be explored through student's group debate and individual paper presentation. Those regions include Northeast Asia, ASEAN, USMCA, EU, Indo-Pacific, Latin America, Middle East, Russia, Africa etc. Contents are South Korea's interactions with major actors in each region such as major issues, challenges and prospects as well as their economic, political and strategic implications for the region and the globe. _As a course for student's international competitiveness, contents are always updated,__such new events as Sino-US new Cold War based on high tech, semiconductors, EVs, critical minerals as well as the impact of Russia-Ukrane War on South Korea and its relations with China, North Korea and Russia_.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
_Course Goals_
* Help students verify sources and build a systemic framework of analysis linking theories of international relations to South Korea's current foreign affairs
* Assist in student's doing independent analysis _based on primary sources and from South Korea's perspective and policy priorities in addition to familiarity with features of_ _South Korea's foreign policy behavior and Korean way of doing business._
_Learning Outcomes_
* Students will be able to independently explore and analyze South Korea's foreign policy behavior and international economic relations from a systemic framework
