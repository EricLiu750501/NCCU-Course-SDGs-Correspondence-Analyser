##  課程簡介Course Description
Henry Kissinger, a world renowned scholar on international politics, a practitioner of American foreign policy, and a decades-
long advisor to the US administrations on diplomacy, passed away in November 2023 at the age of 100. It is the time for any
students who are interested in knowing the major concepts to interpret world order from reading Kissinger's World Order of 
2014. In that book he formulated four concepts, namely European, Chinese, Islamic, and American perspectives to describe
how the world order could be shaped by the above four different political concepts. 
Further to knowing about the world order in the post-Cold War era from a cultural perspective, students are also encouraged
to read the key chapters of Samuel Huntington's classic work of The Clash of Civilizations and the Remaking of World Order
originally published in 1996.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The purpose of this course is to give the students a broad sense of knowing about world order, the origins of war and peace in 
human societies. Through reading Henry Kissinger's World Order, students may get more knowledge about the conflicts
among countries from different lenses. Coupled with reading Samuel Huntington's views of clash of civillizations in the world,
students will have another important tool to observe and analyze international relations. Both Kissinger and Huntington
touched upon the Islamic concept of world order and especially why the Middle East or Islamic world becomes a new powder
keg in the post-Cold War world. After reading these works, students may ask themselves how the world order could be better
maintained via a hierarchy system or an equlibrium of multiple units; and how would legitimacy and power be balanced to
ensure the world order.
