##  課程簡介Course Description
Statistics is the quantitative tool on which psychologists rely very much to provide scientific inference about human behaviors. Therefore, this course is a required course for all psychology students. In order to equip students with necessary statistics knowledge, fundamental topics are addressed in two semesters. In the first semester, students will be introudced to the concepts of probability distribution, parameter estimation, hypothesis testing, and some statistical tests for means including z test and t test. Subsequently, the concepts and computation of effect size and power are introduced also, followed by the introduction of Chi-square distribution and its application in the test of independence and goodness of fit. In the second semester, the correlation analysis, regression analysis, and the analysis of variance are the main focus. Students will also be taught how to use the statistical software R to implement all statistical tests in this course.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
This course is aimed to introduce students knowledge of statistics in psychology. The course contents include introduction of probability and distribution, hypothesis testing, t-test, correlations, regression analysis, and ANOVA. Specifically, students will be asked to learn the statistical software R in/after the class, in order to facilitate their understanding of statistics as well as enhance their skills of doing statistical analysis. The textbook is the same as that used in the last semester. Students will be encouraged to use digital learning platform to learn statistics. After learning statistics in this semester, students are expected to be able to know what correlation, regression, and ANOVA are, how to implement these analyses in R, and when to use them.
