##  課程簡介Course Description
Under contemporary international law, peaceful settlement of international disputes is one of the fundamental principles under conventional and customary international law. Among the various means of dispute settlement, judicial and quasi-judicial bodies are at times chosen by states, and the decisions delivered by such bodies contribute significantly to the development of international law. 
This course aims to provide an overview of international courts and tribunals and their operation, as well as more detailed examination and discussion on selected areas of international law, including statehood, territory, law of the sea, human rights, environment, use of force, law of armed conflict, and international criminal law. Issues of procedural nature, such as jurisdiction and admissibility, are essential components of international jurisprudence and are thus also incorporated in this course.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
  * Present an overview of international courts and tribunals, including their establishment, access, jurisdiction, and various aspects of their operation.
  * Discuss the role of international courts and tribunals in the development of international law in selected areas and examine relevant jurisprudence.
  * Provide students with the opportunity to learn the following skills/abilities: 
    * International legal research
    * International law cases comprehension and briefing
    * Identification of key issues of contention in international disputes
    * Analysis and comparison of cases with similar issues
    * Reflection and critique of existing jurisprudence
