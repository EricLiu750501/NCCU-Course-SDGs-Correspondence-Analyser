##  課程簡介Course Description
This course, Intermediate Accounting II, is the continuation of the Intermediate Accounting series. It focuses on advanced financial accounting topics, including current and non-current liabilities, equity, dilutive securities and earnings per share, investments, accounting for income taxes, accounting changes, and error analysis, and the statement of cash flows.
Through the course, students will develop a deeper understanding of these critical accounting areas and their applications in financial reporting. The course emphasizes the interpretation and application of accounting standards, equipping students with the skills to prepare and analyze complex financial transactions and disclosures. It also provides the foundational knowledge necessary for advanced accounting courses and professional practice in the field of accounting.
The course materials are delivered through video lectures, readings, and problem-solving exercises, with assessments including quizzes and exams to reinforce and evaluate learning. By the end of the course, students will be well-prepared to tackle advanced accounting challenges and apply their skills effectively in both academic and professional settings.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
This course focuses on building a strong foundation in intermediate financial accounting, aiming to enhance students’ ability to apply accounting concepts and practices in various scenarios. By engaging with the course materials, students will develop a deeper understanding of financial reporting, the preparation and analysis of financial statements, and the application of accounting standards to real-world situations.
Upon completing the course, students will be able to:
  1. Apply Accounting Standards : Demonstrate the ability to interpret and apply intermediate-level accounting standards to record and report financial transactions accurately.
  2. Prepare Financial Statements : Develop the skills to prepare and analyze comprehensive financial statements, including income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.
  3. Evaluate Complex Transactions : Gain the ability to assess and account for complex financial transactions, such as revenue recognition, leases, and financial instruments.
  4. Enhance Analytical Skills : Strengthen critical thinking and analytical skills to evaluate financial data and make informed decisions based on financial information.
  5. Prepare for Advanced Accounting : Build a solid foundation for advanced-level accounting courses and professional applications in financial accounting.
Through consistent effort and active engagement with the course materials, students will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to succeed in their academic and professional accounting journeys.
