##  課程簡介Course Description
This course is service-oriented and aims to provide high school students interested in international affairs with a deeper understanding of diplomacy through student-led organization and activity design. During the preparation process, students will not only develop organizational skills, empathy, and other abilities but also reflect on and share their learning experiences in the Department of Diplomacy, achieving the goal of passing down departmental knowledge and experience. The service for this course takes place during the summer vacation each year, while the preparation period occurs during the second semester of the academic year
Learning Content Includes:
  1. Theories and practice of volunteer service
  2. Service project and resource integration
  3. Group dynamics and empathy
Service Content Includes:
  1. Promotion and recruitment
  2. Course and activity design and planning
  3. Design and production of promotional posters
  4. Budget preparation and expense verification
  5. Rental and registration of equipment and venues
  6. Compilation of event handbooks
  7. Environmental cleaning and maintenance

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
  * Through the planning and execution of the summer camp, the course cultivates students’ sense of responsibility, diligence, cooperation, and dedication. Due to time management and the need for team cohesion, the course is primarily intended for students in the Department of Diplomacy. It is strongly recommended that students outside the department refrain from selecting this course.
  * The camp is designed for high school students who are interested in international affairs and aspire to pursue careers in diplomacy. Hosted by the Department of Diplomacy for over 20 years, the workshop includes activities such as guest lectures, negotiation games, diplomatic receptions, and visits to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Through designing and preparing for the workshop, participating students gain practical experience in diplomatic principles and operations, learn about real-world diplomatic challenges, and develop enthusiasm for international affairs as well as collaborative attitudes when working with diplomatic peers.
  * Through a semester-long training, students will gain a foundational understanding of international affairs workshops and volunteer services. They will also design their own international affairs workshop activities, which will help Department of Diplomacy students understand and engage in international volunteer service.
