##  課程簡介Course Description
The course is designed to equip Ph.D. students with a comprehensive framework for analyzing corporate financial policies and corporate governance practices. It aims to deepen their understanding by exploring key theoretical models and providing exposure to advanced empirical methodologies. Through critical evaluation of empirical evidence across a range of topics, students will develop the skills necessary to conduct independent research and contribute to ongoing debates in corporate finance.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The primary goal of this course is to equip Ph.D. students with a deep understanding of contemporary corporate finance research, including its key themes, methodologies, and emerging frontiers. Students will critically engage with the academic literature by preparing referee reports, enabling them to assess the strengths and limitations of scholarly work. They will also develop research creativity by proposing original research ideas grounded in existing knowledge. Through empirical replication exercises, students will enhance their methodological and analytical skills, fostering the ability to conduct independent, high-quality research in corporate finance.

### Referee Report:
The referee report should be 3-5 double-spaced pages, focusing on both the strengths and weaknesses of the paper under review. It should provide constructive feedback and offer suggestions for improvement. Articles for review will be assigned at a later date.
### Research Proposal:
The research proposal should include a comprehensive literature review, clearly defined hypotheses, intended contributions, and a detailed methodology. The proposal must present an original idea rather than replicate existing research. While there is no specific length requirement, the completed proposal must be submitted with a signed “Assignment Submission Declaration,” which will be provided later.
### Replication Report:
The replication report aims to help students understand the complexities of assembling and analyzing a data set. Each student will replicate a test or table from one of the papers studied during the course. A different paper must be selected, subject to prior approval. The report should outline the challenges encountered and explain any differences between the original and replicated results.
