##  課程簡介Course Description
```
This course in econometrics is designed for first-year Economics PhD and Master students. As a continuation of Econometrics I in the fall semester, this course builds on core topics in modern econometric methods and theory.
We will cover topics that are both fundamental and relevant to modern research and practice in economics. Primary topics are based mostly on linear models, including large-sample asymptotics and asymptotic theory of least squares, restricted estimation, hypothesis testing, instrumental variables (IV), generalized methods of moments (GMM), and discrete choice models if time permits.
Emphasis will be placed on developing intuition and understanding the insights behind theoretical results to facilitate their practical application. We will also explore the interplay between econometric theory, methods, and their applications.
```

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The objective of this course is to provide a foundation for applied research in economics. The prerequisite is a fundamental knowledge of linear and matrix algebra, calculus, statistics, and econometrics at the undergraduate level. 
Participants are also assumed to have a knowledge of econometric theory at the level of Econ Master/PhD Econometrics I (roughly Chapters 1-5 of Bruce Hansen's _Econometrics_).
