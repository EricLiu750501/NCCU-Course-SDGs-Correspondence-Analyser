##  課程簡介Course Description
Macroeconomics is a year-long course (3 credits for each semester). The course aims to introduce students to modern macroeconomics and related theories and empirical data under a consistent analytical framework from the short run, the medium run, to the long run. 
The course outline includes: 
1st Semester  
 A tour of the world and Taiwan   
 What is Macroeconomics?   
 Indices of the macroeconomic variable.   
 Goods market (simple Keynesian model)   
 Financial market   
 IS-LM model (complete Keynesian model)   
 Labor market
 The Phillips Curve, the Natural Rate of Unemployment and Inflation  
 IS-LM-PC model 
2nd Semester:  
 Facts and Big Questions of Economic Growth 
 Neoclassical Growth Theory (Solow growth model)   
 Technological Progress and Endogenous Growth   
 Expectations   
 Open Economy
 Monetary and Fiscal Policies

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
Macroeconomics studies the aggregate behavior of a country or an economy. Topics include the theory of the determination of national income, employment, prices, and interest rates; monetary and fiscal policy; related issues of economic growth, fluctuations, inflation, and unemployment; exchange rate and international trade.
The course aims to equip students with knowledge of modern macroeconomic theories and how to apply the approach to the real world using quantitative data.
