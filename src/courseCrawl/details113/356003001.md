##  課程簡介Course Description
This doctoral-level seminar course invites students to engage directly with renowned researchers and scholars from various subfields within Information Management. Over 16 weeks, guest speakers will present their cutting-edge research, providing insight into foundational theories and novel advancements in data science, knowledge management, digital transformation, artificial intelligence, and decision support systems. The course fosters an academic environment that encourages active discussion, critical evaluation, and the exploration of interdisciplinary research trends. Through this exposure, students will gain a deeper understanding of current research methodologies, challenges, and innovations that define the field today.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
Objectives:
  1. To introduce doctoral students to the latest research and advancements in Information Management.
  2. To provide a platform for students to connect with leading researchers, fostering potential collaborations and networks.
  3. To support students in identifying potential research gaps and methodologies relevant to their doctoral research.
Learning Outcomes:
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
1. Critically evaluate contemporary research papers and presentations within the Information Management domain.
2. Identify and articulate emerging trends, theories, and research methods relevant to their areas of interest.
3. Apply insights from guest lectures to refine their research questions and methodologies.
4. Engage in scholarly discussions, demonstrating an ability to question, synthesize, and propose new ideas informed by the latest developments in Information Management.
5. Build connections with experienced researchers that may lead to collaborative research opportunities.
