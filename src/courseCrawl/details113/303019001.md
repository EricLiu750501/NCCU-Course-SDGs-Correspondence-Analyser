##  課程簡介Course Description
This course aims to provide students with a deep and practical understanding of financial statement analysis and corporate valuation, ultimately enabling them to become professional users of financial statements. Through an analytical framework, students will learn to assess and interpret financial statement information, integrating these insights to verify and review corporate strategy. The course covers essential valuation tools and techniques widely used in practice, offering students the opportunity to evaluate firm performance and forecast future prospects. In addition to technical capabilities, the course discusses and emphasizes the ethical responsibilities inherent in financial reporting and analysis, highlighting the importance of integrity and transparency when making reporting choices and business decisions. 
To enhance students’ learning experience, this course combines a theoretical framework with practical applications. Case studies and real-world financial analysis will provide students with hands-on experience, strengthening their analytical and critical thinking abilities while simulating the challenges faced by corporate executives and professionals in business decision-making. Additionally, class discussions and interactive activities will help students develop the ability to communicate financial insights effectively to a diverse range of stakeholders, including investors, management, analysts and other decision-makers.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
After completing this course, you will be able to:
  * Understand the analytical framework used for financial statement analysis and corporate valuation; 
  * Comprehend the accounting practices firms use to play the numbers game;
  * Apply relevant and practical tools and techniques to perform financial statement analysis and corporate valuation;
  * Relate analyzed financial information to critically assess corporate strategy as well as a firm’s performance, prospect, and value;
  * Use analyzed financial information to facilitate more informed decision-making; 
  * Understand the challenges and ethical issues corporate executives face in the decision-making process; and
  * Enhance both oral and written communication skills.
