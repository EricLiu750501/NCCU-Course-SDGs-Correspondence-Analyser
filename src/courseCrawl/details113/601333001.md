_二、課程簡介_
日本對於我們來說，是個既熟悉又陌生的國度。同位於東亞，卻有很多和台灣相異的文化現象，這些文化現象的形成，常源自於日本的歷史和社會的發展，而這些歷史背景和社會現象，也影響了日本的法制度和法解釋論。同時，法制度和解釋論，也同樣影響著社會發展走向。
身為學習和研究法律的我們，透過各種法律規定和實務判決，可以更深刻、更獨到地觀察日本社會，也有機會藉由觀察這個鄰近的國度，反思台灣的法律和社會。
本課程名為「日本民法入門」，課程中介紹日本民法重要基礎概念，並藉由日本的判決，更深入理解日本法的規定在日本社會中的適用狀況。
本課程由學習院大學Daniel Machado准教授、張韻琪助理教授共同授課。由Machado准教授為主要授課教師，以全英語進行課程，本院教師作為中師進行輔助。Machado准教授出身巴西，自高中畢業後於日本求學，除了對於日本社會的洞察深刻之外，以具有以英語教授日本家族法、侵權行為法、財產法之豐富經驗。
本課程是為期10日的集中課程，每次上課進行一個主題，每個主題都會先由授課教師進行50分鐘的講解、第2個50分鐘閱讀一則判決進行討論，第3個50分鐘則由同學們分組討論並簡單報告，如果相關問題發生在台灣如何處理，比較和發現日台異同，並批判性思考制度之差異可能帶來的結果。
適合修習本課程的同學有：尋找碩士論文中比較法素材的碩士班同學、有計畫赴日留學或交換的同學、有興趣於未來從事會使用日本法相關工作的同學、或單純對於日本或日本法有興趣的同學。
Japan is a country both familiar and unfamiliar to us. Although it is located in East Asia like Taiwan, it has many distinct cultural traditions. These traditions often stem from Japan’s unique history and social development, which have influenced its law. Meantime, that same Japanese law also shapes Japanese society.
As students and researchers of law, we can gain a deeper and more insightful understanding of Japanese society through its legislation, legal interpretation and court decisions. Observing this neighboring country also offers us a chance to reflect on Taiwan’s legal and social systems.
This course, titled "Introduction to Japanese Civil Law," covers the essential fundamental concepts of Japanese civil law. By examining Japanese court decisions, students will gain a deeper understanding of how legal provisions are applied within Japanese society.
The course is co-taught by Daniel Machado, Associate Professor of Japanese civil law at Gakushuin University, and Assistant Professor Chang Yunchi. Professor Machado, the primary instructor, conducts the course entirely in English, with Professor Chang providing supplementary support along the class. Originally from Brazil, Professor Machado moved to Japan for his undergraduate studies after high school and has profound insights into Japanese law and society. He is also highly experienced in teaching Japanese private law in English.
This intensive 10-day course covers one topic per day. Each topic includes a 50-minute lecture by the instructor, followed by a 50-minute reading and discussion of a judicial decision, and concludes with a 50-minute student group discussion and presentation. Students will contemplate on how similar cases would be handled in Taiwan, compare and analyze the differences between Japanese and Taiwanese law, and critically assess the potential effects of these legal differences.
This course is well-suited for graduate students seeking comparative law materials for their master’s thesis, graduate and undergraduate students planning to study in Japan, and also students interested in careers involving Japanese law, or anyone simply interested in Japan or Japanese law.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
本課程為日本民法入門的課程，想定的參與學生為對於我國民法已有一定基礎知識的大學部高年級同學、碩士班同學。
課程中精選日本民法中各種重要的基本概念，包含行為能力、公序良俗、意思主義的物權變動、善意第三人保護制度、一般侵權責任之要件、損害賠償範圍、為他人負責的制度、事實上夫妻和內緣關係、雙重婚生推定、法定繼承與遺囑。以上的概念和制度設計，都和我國民法相似卻不盡相同。
修課同學藉由掌握以上幾個日本民法關鍵概念，以及他們在實務判決的適用狀況、學說討論之現狀，可以掌握進一步學習、深入研究日本民法的關鍵，並可以體會到比較法、學習異文化的魅力。
This course is an introduction to Japanese civil law, intended for upper-level undergraduate students and graduate students with a foundational knowledge of Taiwanese civil law.
The course covers a curated selection of essential concepts in Japanese civil law, including legal capacity, public order and morality, the intent-based property transfer system, protections for bona fide third parties, elements of general tort liability, scope of damages, vicarious liability, de facto marital relationships, dual presumptions of legitimacy, statutory inheritance, and wills. While these concepts and legal provisions share similarities with Taiwanese civil law, there are also many significant differences.
By mastering these foundational concepts of Japanese civil law, understanding their application in court decisions, students will gain the ticket to further study and research in Japanese civil law. This course also offers students a rich opportunity to engage in comparative law and a chance to experience a different legal culture.

第1日 導論與簡介：日本民法的歷史
簡要介紹日本法的歷史，重點為介紹日本民法典的制定、結構、母法和修法動向。同時，論及使用英文和英美法的概念來討論大陸法、日本法時會碰到的挑戰。
第2日 主題一：一般法律原則
介紹日本民法典的一般規則，重點放在：第一，民事上的能力（限制行為能力），第二，意思表示和法律行為，第三，濫用權利和違反公序良俗等一般法律原則。
案例研究一：未成年少女在酒店消費，是契約違反公序良俗無效，或因限制行為能力而效力未定？
第3日 主題二：物權法的一般規則
介紹日本物權法一般規則、時效取得制度、登記制度，重點放在：日本民法第177條之解釋問題。
案例研究二：不動產時效取得和登記
第4日 主題三：財產和契約 
介紹日本法中的合意和契約之瑕疵、簡介債權法修法，以及日本實務類推適用第94條第2項等方式，以保護信賴外觀之第三人，並補強有缺陷的登記制度的做法。
案例研究三：類推適用第94條第2項和登記
第5日 主題四：侵權行為之1 
介紹日本一般侵權行為之基本規則，重點放在學說和判決中對於「權利侵害」要件的解釋論的變化。
案例研究四：錯讀韓國人姓名事件
第6日 主題五：侵權行為之2 
介紹日本民法中損害的概念、生命權侵害時損害賠償之繼承可能性、損害的類型、損害賠償額計算中投影出的構造性不平等。 
案例研究五：年輕女孩的餘命損害
第7日 主題六：侵權行為之3 
介紹日本特殊侵權行為的規則、侵權行為能力、少子高齡社會中幼童和高齡者侵權之問題。 
案例研究六：JR東海事件中的為他人負責侵權責任
第8日 主題七：親屬法之1
介紹日本法中的結婚和離婚，重點在於「法定婚姻」和「事實上婚姻（亦稱內緣）」的概念，以及不同類型的事實上婚姻中反映出的新時代和舊時代價值觀之衝突（或可說是自由與多樣性的衝突）。
案例研究七：同性伴侶的事實上婚姻
第9日 主題八：親屬法之2
介紹日本民法中親子關係的成立、日本父母權威之變化，以及日本親子法的最新修法動向，即2022年民法修正案、2024年日本民法修正案。
案例研究八：婚生推定和無婚生推定子女
第10日 主題九：繼承法
簡介日本繼承法的規則、無遺囑繼承與有遺囑繼承之關係、遺囑自由與社會習俗之間的衝突、2018年繼承法修法。 
案例研究九：以「破毀」來撤銷遺囑
Day 1 Orientation and Introduction: History of Japanese Civil Law
Brief history of Japanese Law with a focus on the Japanese Civil Code(JCP): creation, structure, comparative origins, and changes. Also brief comment on the challenges and issues of discussing (and teaching) “Civil Law” in a “Common-law”-oriented language.
Day 2 Topic 1: General Rules
Introduction to the general rules of the JCP with a focus on (1) civil capacity (limited capacity), (2) the notion of manifestation of intention and juridical act, and (3) general principles of law such as abuse of rights and violation of public policy
Case Study 1: Nullity by Violation of Public Policy or Annulment by Limited Capacity
Day 3 Topic 2: General Rules and Property
Introduction to the prescription system, general rules of property, and the registration system with a focus on the interpretive issues surrounding Article 177 of the JCP.
Case Study 2: Acquisition by Prescription and Registration
Day 4 Topic 3: Property and Contracts
Introduction to Vices of Consent and Contracts, Reform of the Law of Obligations (Contract Law), and the balancing of a faulty registration system by the appearances doctrine (Application of Art. 94 (2) by Analogy, etc.)
Case Study 3: Application of Art. 94 (2) by Analogy and Registration
Day 5 Topic 4: Torts 1
Introduction to the general rules of tort liability with a focus on changing case law and legal scholarship about the infringement of rights requirement.
Case study 4: Legal interest to have one’s name pronounced correctly
Day 6 Topic 5: Torts 2
Introduction to the notion of damages, the possibility of inheritance of damages in case of fatal torts, the types of damages, and the issue surrounding the projection of structural inequalities in the assessment of damages.
Case Study 5: Assessing lost profits for the life of a young female 
Day 7 Topic 6: Torts 3
Introduction to special rules of tort liability, the notion of tort capacity, and issues related to the liability for children and elderly people in a shrinking-aging society. 
Case Study 6: JR Tokai Case (Liability for the act of others)
Day 8 Topic 7: Family 1
Introduction to family, marriage and divorce laws in Japan with a focus on the notions of “legal marriage” and “common-law marriage (also termed, de facto marriage)” and the conflicting new and old values (or conflicting freedom and diversity) within the common-law marriage doctrine.
Case Study 7: Same-sex common law marriage
Day 9 Topic 8: Family 2
Introduction to the formation of parent-child relations, the changing notion of parental authority and recent changes of the rules on parent-child relations in Japan with a focus on the 2022 amendment and the 2024 amendment of the JCP. 
Case Study 8: Presumed-non-presumed child
Day 10 Topic 9: Inheritance
Introduction to the law of inheritance with a focus on the relationship between intestate Inheritance and testate Inheritance, the conflict between freedom of will and old customs, and the 2018 inheritance law reform.
Case Study 9: Revoking a Will by “Destruction”
