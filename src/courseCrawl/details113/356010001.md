##  課程簡介Course Description
This is a required course for doctoral students in the department of MIS. Students will be exposed to advanced quantitative methods employed by researchers in information systems, operations management, and decision sciences. The first part of this class (5 weeks) will cover economic models that can be used to analyze single and multiple agents’ behaviors and strategies. The second part of this class (8 weeks) will give you an overview of econometrics and machine learning widely used in empirical research, particularly observational data studies. 
This is a PhD level course so the expectation is high. Both instructors will hold you to high standard and ask you to actively engage in learning. Students will be responsible for paper presentation and discussion occasionally. We assure you this will be a time-consuming yet rewarding experience. Be open-minded to listen to each other, be proactive to share, and think out-of-the-box.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The primary goal of this course is to ensure that students obtain a solid grasp of economic modeling, econometric modeling, and machine learning techniques. We expect students to be able to understand quantitative analyses and results in top journal publications, and more importantly, take a step further to apply the methods to future research projects.
