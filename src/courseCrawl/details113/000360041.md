##  課程簡介Course Description
Statistics is the art and science of using sample data to make generalization about populations. This is the second course of the two-semester sequence (Statistics I & Statistics II) in statistics, and is a continuation of Statistics (I) aiming to provide a comprehensive understanding for the fundamental concepts of statistical inference and data analysis methods. The core topics in this semester will include hypothesis testing for one-sample and two-sample problems, chi-square tests, analysis of variance, regression analysis, and nonparametric methods. ##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The course provides students the underlying foundations of statistics with applications. Upon the completion of this course, students are expected to develop statistical thinking and able to: 1) recognize how hypothesis testing is used to make statistical inferences, 2) familiarize the fundamental concepts of linear regression and correlation and their applications to practical problems, 3) understand and apply the basic principles of nonparametric tests (distribution free tests), and 4) comprehend the groundwork and tools of statistical analysis.
