##  課程簡介Course Description
In today's rapidly evolving engineering and design landscape, digital tools have revolutionized how we conceptualize, develop, and refine complex objects and systems. This course introduces students to the fundamental principles of Computer-Aided Design (CAD), bridging theoretical knowledge with practical, hands-on experience in modern design environments.
CAD is about being able to transform abstract ideas into precise, detailed models that can be analyzed, modified, and brought to life through digital and physical manufacturing. By using CAD software tools, planners, designers, and engineers can:
  * Create 2D and 3D models and maps with precision
  * Simulate and analyze design performance before physical production
  * Rapidly iterate and optimize designs using prototyping
  * Communicate complex ideas through detailed visual representations

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
By the end of this course, students will:
  1. Understand the theoretical foundations of digital design processes
  2. Develop proficiency in a professional 2D and 3D CAD system (AutoCAD 202x)
  3. Learn to model, analyze, and optimize design concepts across various domains
  4. Gain practical skills applicable in planning, architecture, engineering, manufacturing, and beyond
Students will have worked on a number of different 2D and 3D designs and produced technically well-designed maps, construction and engineering plans, and 3D models. Course participants will also have a clear and precise understanding of the overall CAD design process and a variety of topics in computational geometry and modeling. 
Students will be familiar
  * Navigating professional CAD software interfaces
  * Creating complex 2D and 3D models
  * Applying design constraints and parameters
  * Performing structural and performance analyses
  * Preparing designs for 3D printing and manufacturing

This course is designed to be welcoming and approachable for students from all academic backgrounds. Whether you're an engineering student, an architect, a designer, or exploring a new skill from an entirely different field, you'll find a supportive learning environment here.
We understand that many students arrive with no prior experience in Computer-Aided Design (CAD) or vector graphics. This isn't a barrier—the curriculum is specifically structured to:
  * Start from foundational concepts
  * Build skills progressively and systematically
  * Avoid repetitive, monotonous training
  * Develop a versatile, transferable skill set
We will primarily use AutoCAD 202x, a design tool across multiple industries. The software will have both English and Mandarin interface options. The course language will be English.
