##  課程簡介Course Description
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to conducting empirical research in sociology. It is designed to equip students with the foundational skills needed to critically examine and investigate social phenomena and processes through the integration of theoretical frameworks and empirical evidence. Emphasizing the importance of research design and data collection, this course will guide students in formulating research questions, developing hypotheses, and selecting appropriate methodologies for data gathering. While data analysis techniques will be introduced, the primary focus will be on building a solid foundation in designing rigorous studies and collecting high-quality data to address sociological inquiries effectively.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
  * To improve students’ research ability to leverage interactions between theoretical models that clarify complex logical relationships and empirical work that delineates important properties of data generating processes
  * To be able to critically evaluate the strength and weakness of empirical research design in sociology
  * To be familiar with the most up-to-date research and data collection methods in sociology
