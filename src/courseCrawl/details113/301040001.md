##  課程簡介Course Description
As of 2002, Taiwan became a Member to the World Trade Organization (WTO), and obliged to comply with the rules under the WTO covered agreements. This course will discuss relevant international trade rules, ongoing negotiating issues and important trade disputes under the WTO. This course covers the brief introduction to the history and structure of the WTO, and examines various rules regarding trade in goods, services and trade-related intellectual property rights under the covered agreements including the GATT, GATS and TRIPS. Selected WTO dispute settlement cases will also be discussed.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The purpose of this course is to familiarize students with the following points of understanding on the WTO mechanism that underscores the rule-based approach for the solution of trade disputes among WTO Members:
1. History, structure and mandate of the WTO.
2. Basic international trade rules under the GATT, GATS and TRIPS agreements.
3. Major substantive issues involved in selected WTO dispute settlement cases.
4. Important issues in the ongoing negotiation process.
