##  課程簡介Course Description
This course offers comprehensive a guidance on crafting a well-structured thesis, with a focus on essential writing strategies for effectively presenting research and meeting academic standards in the Information Systems (IS) field. Students will learn to develop a clear research question, refine their theoretical argument, select research methodology, and organize complex data systematically so as to gain research insights. Key topics include literature review techniques, theoretical framework development, the integration of evidence and analysis, and the presentation of theoretical and practical contribution. Emphasis is placed on clarity, conciseness, and cohesion to enhance readability and impact within the IS discipline. By the end of the course, students will be equipped with practical skills to create a polished, compelling thesis that reflects rigorous academic standards and contributes meaningfully to their field of study.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
  1. Develop a Clear Research Focus
Students will be able to articulate a focused research question or thesis statement, refining it to align with academic standards and ensuring it provides a strong foundation for their study.
  1. Managing Thesis Structure and Organization
Students will learn to structure their thesis logically, organizing content into coherent sections (such as introduction, literature review, methodology, analysis, and conclusion) that effectively convey their research findings and arguments.
  1. Enhance Academic Writing Clarity and Precision
Students will strengthen their ability to write clearly, concisely, and precisely, with a focus on eliminating ambiguity, reducing redundancy, and ensuring that each section contributes to the overall thesis argument.
