##  課程簡介Course Description
This course applies economic thinking and several recent studies to analyze real estate market fundamentals and bubbles. It deeply examines the factors that affect the demand for and supply of real estate markets from the macro and micro perspectives. This course also explores alternative theories (behavioral economics) for real estate price movements and the causes and impact of the cyclical nature of the real estate industry. The theoretical framework describes the operation of the different real estate markets and can apply to any free economy (country), including the local market. The first part of the course will focus on how to use traditional economics to analyze real estate markets. In the second part of the course, we take a closer look at the current development of international real estate markets. In the final part, we examine real estate markets from the behavioral economics perspective.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
Students will acquire a good grasp of the economic concepts and theories needed for the analysis of real estate markets and also gain an in-depth understanding of the factors and the dynamics that drive the operation of real estate markets. Students taking this course will be expected to:
1. Develop a theoretical foundation of economics of real estate markets, with particular emphasis on the housing markets.
2. Develop an understanding of current international real estate markets.
3. Develop an understanding of the latest economic theories, which are beyond the fundamentals of real estate markets.
