##  課程簡介Course Description
This postgraduate course focuses primarily on Leibniz’s _Monadology_ and Fichte’s commentary on Leibnizian themes in his _Lectures on Logic and Metaphysics_. Leibniz’s philosophy exerted immense influence on the German academic tradition of the 18th century. However, the reception and critical transformation of his philosophy during the era of German Idealism remain relatively underexplored. This course addresses this gap by closely examining Leibniz’s _Monadology_ and related texts (using Nicholas Rescher’s acclaimed edition) alongside Fichte’s Jena lectures on logic and metaphysics, which reflect his critical engagement with the neo-Leibnizian tradition.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
  1. Demonstrate a thorough understanding of Leibniz’s metaphysical views and their discussion in Fichte’s Jena lectures.
  2. In their papers, students will be expected to identify and analyze key metaphysical issues in classical German philosophy, articulating their understanding and critical assessments in their own terms.
