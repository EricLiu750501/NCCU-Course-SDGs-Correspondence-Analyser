##  課程簡介Course Description
The course investigates various aspects of poetry and their philosophical and political implications. A range of poetic genres and forms will be discussed in class, with specific emphasis on a historical period, an individual poet, and a close study of a poetic form. The primary goal of the course is to familiarize students with various ways to evaluate, analyze, and enjoy poetry reading. It also aims to help students become more engaged, curious, and sensitive readers of poetry and to increase their confidence as writers thinking about poetic texts. Furthermore, it hopes to provide students with the scholarly language to conduct literary research about poetry. Eventually, the course will assist in developing students's close reading of poetry and strengthening their capacity to position poetry reading in larger contemporary social, historical, political, and theoretical frameworks.

##  課程目標與學習成效Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes 
The course intends to explore the nature of poetry and its various formalistic manifestations. It draws students' attention to a number of elements in poetry, including voice, imagery, sound, rhythm, and form in poetic communication. It introduces students to the current critical discussion about the concepts of poetry and helps them figure out various ways to explain and interpret poems. The ultimate goal of the course is to see how various approaches to analyzing poetic formality and musical elements serve as a powerful way to engage with society. It aims to show how poetry reading connects students to other aspects of our contemporary living, thinking, and acting.
